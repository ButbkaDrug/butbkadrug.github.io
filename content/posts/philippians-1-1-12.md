### Back to Paul and Timothy
Same with Paul's letter. He writes:

### Philippians 1:1
> ¹Paul and Timothy, bond-servants of Christ Jesus...

We can observe that Paul not only mentions himself. But also Timothy. Timothy was Paul's companion when they have started the church in Philippi. People in the church knew Timothy well for as long as they knew Paul. Also, it let's them know that they are still working together. After 10 years of ministry.

### Bond Servants
Next, what we see in the text is Paul saying "bond-servants". In Greek it would be [ doulos ]. Which literally means "slaves".

...

Interesting... *Paul and Timothy* slaves?*

Why from all possible ways to speak about them, Paul choses this one?

One might think that it's not important. That it's just a figure of speach, a metaphor, litrary techniq of some sort. But I can assure you it is not. I'm sure that it was very delibirat choice. **Paul makes an important statement.**

He choses the lowes rank in Roman society and assosiete himself with it. Slaves were considered more of a property then people. They had no rights. No part in civic life. No respect. Ther were unfathomable gap between Roman citizents and slaves. Roman citizents were pround of their status. Pride was big part of the society. And it transfered to the church as well.

**Paul sets the example of humility.** Disregardig his status of Apostle, Roman citizent, Jewish rabby. Assosiating with a lowes rank possible - slaves.

In other words Paul says: "Hey, it Me and Timothey, and we consider ourselves nothing more but the slaves".

### Paul and Timothy
Another interesting observation is that he writes **Paul and Timothy Bond servants**... And again it is a bit different from his other letters. If would look the second Corinthians would see:

### 2 Corinthians 1:1
> ¹Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by the will of God, and *our* brother Timothy...

See the difference with letter to Philippians?

In the letter to Corinth you can see big differance betwen who is Paul and who is Timothy.

In the letter to Philippians Paul puts both himself and Timothy on the same level of authority. Not by accident. Not because he was in a good mood or something. It was thought out.

Paul was highly regarded in the church. People looked up to him as an example. With great respect and honor. Both He and Timothy started the church. Most likely Paul appointed elders and deacons. They knew about Pauls ministry for the Lord. He was an outstanding person and they knew it.

By putting himself and Timothy at the same level. Paul brings himself down in the eyes of people from the church. Because Paul and Timothy was not at the same level of authority. Also, most lickly Timothy was not roman citizent. Which makes this statement even more radical for listeners. Even more so when Paul assosiate both of them with slaves. 

**Paul sets the example of humility.**

### Answer
And the answer is qute simple. At this point Paul was aware of the issue they've had in the church. Anisimus, the guy who brought the money, gave him heads up. And Paul was making sure that oversiers and diakons will pay attention to his words as well as everybody else. 

And don't think of themselves as those who already reached the fullness. And those instructions are not for them, but for mere mortals. Regual christians.

That is why Paul specifically calls them out. Saing "Overseers and deacons please, listen I'm speaking to you as well."

### Affirmation
Paul continues with some affirmation:

### Philippians 1:3-6
> ³I thank my God in all my remembrance of you,⁴always offering prayer with joy in my every prayer for you all,⁵in view of your participation in the gospel from the first day until now.

>⁶*For I am* confident of this very thing, that He who began a good work among you will complete it by the day of Christ Jesus.

In verses 3-5 he praises Philippians for their faithful participation in the gospel. Which I read like Philippians were doers. Faithfull followers and workers. Who would apply the word in their life. And also promote gospel further. They had no problems with that.

But verse 6 server as a reminder. That the work is not finished yet. That no one will reached completion. That God continue to work in us until the day of Christ Jesus. In other words none of you is perfect yet, but God will make you perfect on THAT day.
