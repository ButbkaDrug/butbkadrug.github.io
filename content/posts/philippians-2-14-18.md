### INTRO
Our passage for today brings together everything Paul have talked about in first two chapters. It might seem like there is a lot going on, but really if we would zoom out, and look at the text from higher level. We would immediately recognize that there is only one main topic. Which is **encouragement**... Encouragement to continue to live for Christ.

### BACK STORY - THE PAUL
In case you missed\forgot story that proceeds this letter. I'll remind you of couple things. Paul, the author of the letter, was a key figure for the church he wrote the letter to. Very honorable, highly respected. Modeling role for many. Paul started their church. He brought many of them to faith. In fact he was **the pioneer**, **main promoter**, **chief prophet** of this new movement **"salvation by faith for the Gentiles"**. Apostle Paul dedicated his entire life to the ministry. Selflessly serving God for many years. He was one of the most, if not the most dedicated, committed and faithful person the church have ever known.

They saw how passionate he is, and that made **them** passionate. They shared in his ministry, his vision, his work. For years they were working together.

Today we call people like Paul - **influencers**. Those who have millions of subscribers on the social platforms. A lot of people look up to them. Believe in what they say without a doubt. All that to say, Paul made an impact on their lives.

He was **the Paul**. Unstoppable, fearless and determined. Paul who would get stoned and just refuse to die, but go back into the city and continue to preach. Who survived countless assassination attempts. Always on the move looking for the new frontiers. It felt like nothing could stop him from going places preaching the Gospel.

> ...

### NEW REALITY
But then something happened. Something broke, something went wrong. **The Paul**, invincible and unstoppable, now is under arrest. Not for some short time, few night and what not. Like it was many times before. No, this time it is for real. This time they've got him. Last few years Paul spent being escorted from prion to prison. Facing potential death sentence... No miraculous release, no nothing. Not anymore.

Besides that Philippians themselves got in trouble because of their faith. We don't know exactly what was going on with them, but again, stuff was real. In the letter Paul says: 

### Philippians 1:30 (NIV)
> "...you are going through the same struggle you saw I had, and now hear that I still have."

Most likely tensions with authorities. Very likely some people got arrested and now in prison...

I think no one will argue that this can be disappointing. Considering Paul's situation you might start wondering. **Is that what you get by being faithful servant with all your life?** **Maybe all this faith business is fake and all that we had to endure was for nothing?**

People were worried, disappointed and confused. 

Their enemies would probably say things like: 

> "Your Paul is a mere criminal. No doubt he'll get executed soon, and then we will come after you too! You fools, got deceived by some Jew."

## DISAPPOINTMENT
Disappointing! They have joined this new movement, that looked very promising. Heavily invested into this **new faith**. And now, decade later, it all stats to fall apart. Thought leader is arrested. They have gain some powerful enemies along the way. Probably not what you would expect,right? It might get you disappointed and discouraged. It might make you start questioning your decisions.

...

That was my slightly dramatized(or maybe not) version of what was going on. Fully based on the scriptures. In the situation like that you would use some encouragement, some clarification.

Fist two chapters of the letter do exactly that. They encourage people to continue to pursue the life with God.

### DISCREDITATION
Firstly Paul addresses attempts to **DISCREDIT HIM** as a messenger, and therefore **the message**:

### Philippians 1:12-14
 ¹²Now I want you to know, brothers *and sisters,* that my circumstances have turned out for the greater progress of the gospel,¹³so that my imprisonment in *the cause of* Christ has become well known throughout the praetorian guard and to everyone else,¹⁴and that most of the brothers *and sisters,* trusting in the Lord because of my imprisonment, have far more courage to speak the word of God without fear.

From this text we can clearly see that Paul's sufferings are not caused by the lack of faith. Or because he was yet to unlock the secrets of prosperity. No... bible says that Paul is in prison, **not because he is a bad Christian*, but  BECAUSE he is actually a **real one**. There is no legitemend charges again him, accept for his faith in Christ. So, there is nothing wrong he did wrong. Nothing wrong with him as a messenger, therefore there is nothing wrong with the message he preaches too!

Bible points out that Paul's circumstances, which were intended to stop the spread of the gospel by his enemies, actually served to further advancement of it. His sufferings are not discredit the gospel in any way. In verse 20 Paul say:

### Philippians 1:20
²⁰...my eager expectation and hope, that I will not be put to shame in anything, but *that* with all boldness, Christ will even now, as always, be exalted in my body, whether by life or by death.

His suffering do not indicate that he stepped out of God's will. Fall into sin. Or made a mistake. **PAUL'S SUFFERING bring as much glory to God as the other stages of his ministry**.

### First Encouragement
If you experiencing some form of the suffering today. Whether it is decease, financial struggle or some other form of suffering in your life it does not immediately mean that God is not with you. That you have lack of faith. You're not spiritual enough. Didn't read enough bible, didn't prayed enough. Didn't pray the **right** prayers. Probably should have fasted too. Or maybe that you simply not born again at all.

No, sometimes sufferings just a part of the journey. You should not get discouraged in your faith if you experiencing some struggles. Or if your role model, someone you look up to in terms of faith experiencing some struggles. It should not serve as discouragement for you. 

### Continue to pursue faith.
In his letter to the church Paul says **regardless of what will happen to me, you should continue to live your life for God**.

### Ownership
The end of the chapter one and the whole chapter two talk about it. That we should take ownership over our faith. Because, let's face it. Sometimes we do things because of other people. 

### GYM
You can go into the gym, because of your friends or because your important person goes there. And you kind of cruse along. Their passion ignites for fire in you too. But, deep inside you know, that you will never do it on your own. The day they will stop going you will stop too. Because, it was actually never your passion.

Same can be with our christian faith. And we talked about it few weeks ago. Sometimes we just fallow someone else's faith, passion for God. But the time comes when we will need to take the ownership over our faith. When we will believe even if everyone else will fail. When we will continue to walk with the Lord even if no one else by our side. Even if our key person is gone.

...

### Passage
For all those, who decided to go till the end, Paul writes following:

### Philippians 2:14-1
> ¹⁴Do all things without complaining or arguments;¹⁵so that you will prove yourselves to be blameless and innocent, children of God above reproach in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you appear as lights in the world,¹⁶holding firmly the word of life, so that on the day of Christ I can take pride because I did not run in vain nor labor in vain.¹⁷But even if I am being poured out as a drink offering upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice and share my joy with you all.¹⁸You too, *I urge you,* rejoice in the same way and share your joy with me.


### ALL THINGS
This passage starts with "Do all things". What does it mean "all things"? This words are conclusion of the few thoughts Paul expressed earlier. "All things" - ties together chapter one and two. All the advice and edification bible gives there. It directly referencing **verse 12** which say: *Work out your salvation...*.

So, we can say **Work out your salvation without complaining or arguments**. Without *secret displeasure*, *unhappiness that not openly avowed*. Work out your salvation without without *disputing*, *doubting*.

### WORK OUT YOUR SALVATION - HOW TO DO
This phrases **do all things** or  **work out your salvation** Bring together all that we talk about over the last few month. Chapter 1 talks about **"conduct worthy of the Gospel"**. Living as a **Citizens of Heaven** which includes:

- **TOGETHERNESS** - chapter 1:27: Stand firm in one spirit. Having one mind, striving together for the faith of the Gospel. *Like a solders in the battle.*
- **SELFLESSNESS** - chapter 2:3-5: No selfishness or empty conceit. Considering one another more important then ourselves. Looking out for interest\well being of others, not only our own.
- **CHRIST LIKENESS** - chapter 2: 5- 10: To have same attitude Jesus have. Extreme humility, expressed in extreme obedience.

Those, who take ownership over their faith and over their walk with the Lord. All those who want to please God with their life. For all of us the encouragement and command is - **To Do all this** without secret displeasure and disputing. This is what **working out** your salvation is. Those are "all things" Paul talks about. This is **the will of God** for our as a community and for you as an individual part of that community!

This is a true **life of worship**, an offering we can make for God.

### Philippians 2:15
> ..¹⁵so that you ~will prove yourselves to be~  blameless and innocent, children of God above reproach in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you appear as lights in the world holding firmly the word of life...

I find this translation to be not the best. Here it says" *you will prove yourself to be...* But if we will look into original text, the word there(yes, it's actually only one word in Greek) the word have a meaning of **BECOMING** something you were not before. Entering a new state ~~and it assumes active participation.~~

So, in other words, as you **working out your salvation, without grumbling and arguments. And you **will become** blameless and innocent children of God above reproach...

You will live the kind of life pleases God. When Paul says "without reproach". He borrows the images from the temple service. When people would bring an animal to be offered as a sacrifice to God. It has to be **without reproach**. Without blemish. Only this kind of sacrifice were considered acceptable. It would *please* God.

This is a secret formula of living a life that pleases God. Bible calls it "living sacrifice", "spiritual service of worship(Romans 12:1)".

### Light for the world
As we work out our salvation without grumbling and disputing, It will bring glory to God. It will make us to stand out among people of this world:

### Philippians 2:15-16
> ¹⁵...children of God above reproach in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you appear as lights in the world, holding firmly the word of life...

### GENERATION
*Crooked and perverse generation* means the society that went out of aliment with God and his ways. As we work our our salvation our life will start to emit light for those around us. The other translations say as a starts in the world. In other words we become source of light for our society. For the people around us. We become the light that help people to navigate in the darkness of this world.


### What is the light

### Philippians 2:15-16
¹⁵...as lights in the world, holding firmly(displaying) the word of life...

We become a light as we **holding firmly** to the word of life. When we continue to pursue relationships with our Father in heaven. As we practice to do life HIS way more and more. Then, our life, our relationships, our attitudes and our words will start to reflect the world of life. 

We shine in this world by putting to display or holding out the word of life. It's not about us, it's about God who works in us. And this is what pleases God. This is a true living sacrifice. 

### Philippians 2:16-18
> ...so that on the day of Christ I can take pride because I did not run in vain nor labor in vain.¹⁷But even if I am being poured out as a drink offering upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice and share my joy with you all.¹⁸You too, *I urge you,* rejoice in the same way and share your joy with me.

It's a final note of encouragement. Pinnacle of the fist two chapters. There are several things we should look at, but today I want us to notice one. And is that according to the bible our faith in God and our service to him is a main sacrifice. Yes, Paul talking about his potential death, about him being poured out as a *drink offering*. And again it's a images from temple service. And a drink offering is more like an addition to a main sacrifice. Like a cheery on top of the cake.

But the cake is our walk with the Lord.

### CONCLUSION
Today we looked at 3 main point's of the first two chapters. 

Firstly Paul explained us **What to do**?
    * Conduct worthy of the Gospel
    * Working out our Salvation
    * Being Christ minded

Then he explained **How to do it**?
    * To all things without complaining and arguments.

And at last he explained **Why it is important**?
    * It pleases and glorifies God
    * That is a true sacrifice
