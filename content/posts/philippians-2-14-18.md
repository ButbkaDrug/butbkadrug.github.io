### INTRO

Our passage for today brings together everything Paul have talked about in this two chapter. If we would zoom out, and look at this chapters from far away. We would immediately recognize that the main goal here is encouragement. Encouragement to continue to live for God.

Let me remind you of couple things. Paul was a key figure for this church. Very honorable, highly respected. Modeling role. He started their church. He brought them to faith. He was the pioneer and main promoter of the idea "salvation for Gentiles". He dedicated his life to ministry, selflessly serving God for many years. He was probobly the most dedicated person they have ever knew.

They saw his passion and it made them passionate. They shared in his ministry, his vision, his work. For years.

And what they see now? He is in under arrest. Last several years spent in different prions. And now facing potential death sentence...

And Philippians themselves got in trouble because of their faith. I think no one will argue that this can be disappointing. By look at the Paul's situations and inevitable you will inevitable think - is that what you get for faithfully serving God for all your life? Maybe all this faith business is fake and all that we had to endure is for nothing?

They were worried, disappointed and confused. 

They had enemies in their city. Who would probably say: "Your Paul is a mere criminal. He'll get executed, and soon they will come after you too! You fools, got deceived by some Jew."

### ALL THINGS
This passage starts with "Do all things". This paragraph is a conclusion of the few thoughts Paul expressed earlier. It ties together with **verse 12**, which say: *Work out your salvation...*. So, we can say **Work out your salvation without complaining or arguments**. Without *secret displeasure*, *unhappines that not openly avowed*. Work out your salvation without *divisive arguments*, without *disputing*.

### WORK OUT YOUR SALVATION
Well, what does **work out your salvation means then**? One may ask. We talk about it couple weeks ago. In chapter 1 Paul describes it as **"Conduct worthy of the Gospel"**. Remember, we were talking about living as a **Citizens of Heaven**? We talked that we should not wait until we get to heaven, to act like citizens of heaven. Important points are:

- **TOGETHERNESS** - chapter 1:27: Stand firm in one spirit. Having one mind, striving together for the faith of the Gospel. Like a solders in the battle.
- **SELFLESSNESS** - chapter 2:3-5: No selfishness or empty conceit. Considering one another more important then ourselves. Looking out for interest\well being of others, not only our own.
- **CHRIST LIKENESS** - To have same attitude Jesus have. Extreme humility, expressed in extreme obedience.

Do all this without secret displeasure and disputing. This is what working out your salvation is. This are those "all things" we should do without complaining or arguments. This is the will of God for our as a community and for you as an individual part of the community! That's what the bible encourages us to do.
