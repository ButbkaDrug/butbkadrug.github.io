### Intro.
In our scripture for today, Paul continue to explain his situation to the believers in Philippy. Just as a reminder in case you missed few Sundays, apostle Paul is in a Roman prison. He sends a letter to his friends to say thank you and encourage them. Christians in Philippy loved Paul very much. They were very concerned about him, about his situation. They knew that it can result both ways. In Paul's release as well as his execution.

And if we will imagine something like that happening to our friend. To someone who is very dear to our heart. I think we would also be very concerned. We would want this person to be free, to continue to live.

I think we would not spare our resources to help that person. And this is very right. This is proper attitude. You know, if your friend is in prison. Falsely accused. Potentially facing death sentence and it doesn't bother you at all... I mean, don't get me wrong, but I think something is wrong with you.

**It should bother you!**

In a situations like that, we start having questions: Where is God? Why he's not intervene the situation?

We can get discouraged. More and more questions arise in our minds. It can be challenging for our faith. Isn't it?

### Example
We have remotely similar situation just recently. Our dear brother got detained by immigration service. And end up in immigration prison. Even though he did everything possible on his side to follow the law.

And you know what? We were concerned about him. It did not feel right. It didn't feel like God's will, at least to me it didn't.

Our brother was a good Christian. Faithful minister. He served to unreached people group here in Georgia. Sharing gospel with them. And he were pretty successful. So, logically one would think that nothing like that shouldn't ever happen to him.

### When it happens
And indeed when such thing happens, it almost immediately raises a question: Where is God? Why he doesn't help his faithful minister?

Because obviously it cannot be God's will...

And as situation does not resolve, our faith might grows weak. We might start to doubt God more and more. And if it's results in a bad way, we experience crisis in our faith.

If you familiar with such feelings, Today's scripture will shed some light on what might be happening behind the scenes.

### History
To help us understand the situation we will go back to the book of acts. Where we can see the beginning of the story. Where at the end of his third missionary journey Apostle Paul returns to Jerusalem. And how things starts to evolve:

In Acts 19 we jump to the moment when Paul and his companions reached the goal of their journey. Verse 21:

### Acts 19:21
> ²¹Now after these things were finished, Paul resolved in the Spirit to go to Jerusalem after he had passed through Macedonia(Phillipy) and Achaia, saying, “After I have been there, I must also see Rome.”

In this verse, we see that it was not just Paul's Idea. Text says "Paul resolved in the spirit." Or as other translation states "He felt compelled by this..." So we can see that Paul didn't step out of the wheel of God. It was not a result of his sin. Or anything else. He in fact, followed the spirit of God. And to make it clear, to make sure we understand that it was in fact a plan. God sends his prophet.


And honestly, I think that Profit were sent not so much for Paul, but more for his companions. Prophet prophecy so that me and you as well as companions of Paul and believers would know that God is in it. Acts 21 from the verse 10 we read:

Lord encourages Paul, and this words encourage us, that even if you found yourself in such complicated situation, God is still is in control. Because of his imprisonment, Paul was able to testify, to witness, to share the gospel with several kings. And in the end with the emperor of Rome.

So Paul didn't wanted believers in Philippi to be discouraged because of his imprisonment. We read word "Joy "Multiple times in the first chapter. Which tells us Paul is not depressed about his situation. Even though it's been like five years since he got arrested.
