## Intro.
In our scripture for today, Paul continue to explain his situation to believers in Philippy. If you are new here or for everyone who maybe missed last couple Sundays. I'll remind that we are going through the letter to Philippians. Apostle Paul is in a Roman prison. He sends a letter to his friends to say thank you and encourage them.

Christians in Philippy loved Paul very much. He was an important figure in their lives. He started their church. He wa their spiritual father.

They were very concerned about him and his situation. It has been going for years at this point. And they knew that it can resolve in 2 ways.

1. First option is what they desire and pray for:  Paul's release.

2. Second option was not so appealing. It is Paul's execution.

### Project to our life
Can you imagine something of that sort happening to your friend? To someone who is very dear to your heart?

I think you would also be very concerned. You would want this person to be released. For all accusations to be dropped.

We would not spare our resources to help that person. To speak for them. Pray for them. Provide financial support etc.

And there is nothing wrong with that. I believe that this is proper attitude. You **should** feel this way. And react this way.

You know, if your friend is detained under false accusations. Potentially facing death sentence and it doesn't bother you at all... I mean, don't get me wrong, but I think there is something wrong with you then.

**It should bother you!** You should lose your sleep over it. It should occupy your mind.

And espesially if this person is a commited believer or pastor, missionary etc. We can start having questions: **Where is God?** **Why he's not intervene the situation?**

We can get discouraged in our faith if situation does not quickly resolve. It can be a challenge for our faith. Isn't it?

### Recent Situation
We've had remotely similar situation just recently. Our dear brother got detained by immigration service. And end up in so called "immigration prison". Even though he did everything possible on his side to follow the law.

And you know what? **We were** concerned about him. It did not feel right. It didn't feel like it is the God's will for him. At least to me it didn't.

Our brother was a good Christian. Faithful minister. He served the Lord here in Batumi. He ministered to unreached people group. Sharing gospel with them. And it was pretty successful. So, logically one would think that nothing like that shouldn't ever happen to him. Right?

### When it happens
And indeed when things of such sort happen. It is confusing. It almost immediately raises a question: **Where is God?** **Why he doesn't help his faithful minister?** **Does God's plan failed?** **Got defetead?**

Because in our minds **obviously it cannot be God's will...** for him.

**Or can it?**

If such feelings not foreign to you. In case such questions went through your mind before. I have the good news. Our today's scripture will shed some light on what might be happening behind the scenes. We will see whether it is something that we should be conserned of? And maybe it will give us some clues what we should do in our current situation of ever evolving complications of staying here.

## INRO END

### History
And in order to understand Paul's situation better, we should to go back to the book of acts. We will look at the end of the third missionary journey of an Apostle Paul. At the point in time when things started to evolve:

Let's open **Acts 19** and jump to the verses where Paul and his companions accomplished the goal of their journey. And stated to think what's next. In Verse 21 Apostle Luke writes following:

### Acts 19:21
> ²¹Now after these things were finished, Paul resolved in the Spirit to go to Jerusalem after he had passed through Macedonia( which almost sertanly means through the church in Phillipy) and Achaia, saying, “After I have been there, I must also see Rome.”

### Personal conviction
In this verses, we can see that Paul and the team(and from now one I'll mostly refer to their crew as "Paul", but keep in mind he was not alone) accomplished the goal of their third missionary journey. And, you know, naturally they came to a point when they need to decide what's next? This trip is finished. They did all they wanted to do. So, what do we do now?

Our text says "after this things were finished **Paul resolved in the Spirit**." Or as other translations say "**He felt compelled by the Spirit..."**.

Now, in the new testament when we bible talks about "Holy Spirit" and let's say "spirit of man" it uses the same word **πνεῦμα (pneuma)**. And usually the context is the key to understand wether we talk about Holy Spirit of God. Or we talk about human spirit. Some authors add word *Holy Spirit* but others don't. 

So, in this situation we have two opportunities, two general opinions:
1. Idea to go to Jerusalem came from the Holy Spirit. Or at least it was approved by the Holy Spirit.
2. It was just Paul's idea.

And honestly, as far as our study for today goes. It is almost irrelevent which one is more true. It's all not that important today.

But was is important to understand that trip to Jerusalem, wheter it was a mistake on a Paul's side or not. All the trouble Paul got into was not a result of his sin or anything else. All that have happend to him there was **ONLY** because of his faith in Jesus Christ.

It was not because he didn't pay his taxes. Or been smuggling stuff. Or I don't know... was breaking some laws. No. All the persecusion and accusations, all the hate was only because of his Faith in Jesus. Because of his mission to preach selation to Gentiles.

### The Prophet
And as they continue Towards Jerusalem, Holy Spirit keeps speaking about what will happen. So, Paul does not go unaware. Rather opposite in Act 20 verses 22 and 23 we read:

### Acts 20:22,23
²²And now, behold, bound by the Spirit, I am on my way to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there,²³except that the Holy Spirit solemnly testifies to me in every city, saying that chains and afflictions await me.

And later in chapter 21 when they already reached Tyre Luke writes:
### Acts 21:4
⁴After looking up the disciples, we stayed there for seven days; and they kept telling Paul, through the Spirit, not to set foot in Jerusalem.

And at last when they got even closer to Jerusalem. We read about prophet coming down from Judea in verses 10 and 11 of the same chapter:

### Acts 21:10-11
> ¹⁰...a prophet named Agabus came down from Judea.¹¹And he came to us and took Paul’s belt and bound his own feet and hands, and said, “This is what the Holy Spirit says: ‘In this way the Jews in Jerusalem will bind the man who owns this belt and hand him over to the Gentiles.’

God were speaking a lot about what would happen. I think it was important both for Paul and other people to hear this. His companions and believers in the churches they visit was informed by the Holy Spirit. At this point everybody new what is about to happen. Some people say that Paul was in direct rebellion based on the verse 4 chapter 21. Others tend to think that it was just interpretation of what is Holy Spirit saying rather then Paul's rebel. But again it's not all that important.We will try to look at both possibilities. 

I think that though this prophecies we can see that God is in control. At this point, it was very clear that Paul will get arrested. That bad things will happen to him if he will set his feet into the city. No one have doubt about it.

And when people hear this, they have typical, very normal, very human reaction. Let's continue reading in verse 12:

### Acts 21:12-14
> ”¹²When we had heard this, we as well as the local residents *began* begging him not to go up to Jerusalem.¹³Then Paul replied, “What are you doing, weeping and breaking my heart? For I am ready not only to be bound, but even to die in Jerusalem for the name of the Lord Jesus.”

> ¹⁴And since he would not be persuaded, we became quiet, remarking, “The will of the Lord be done!”

We can see that Paul have made a choice. Very concese, very informed choise. He knew ahead of time what will happen. And no one would judge him if he would decide not to go. In fact, as we can see people bagged him not to go. Not only regular believers. But also apostle Luke and Timothy, his companion and some other mighty men of God, who have been through rough times together with Paul.

### Observation
When we read other places in Acts we can see that few times Holy Spirit not allowed Paul to go places. And Paul didn't. This is how they went to Europe, to Philippy instead of Asia minor as Paul planned. This time situation is different. God wans Paul about trouble. But, I think he leaves the choice up to Paul. Wether to go or not to go.

And Paul already expressed his position in verse 13.

So, I think that we can agree that Paul's imprisonment was not some kind of mistake. Not an error or result of confusion. It was a choice that Paul made. Path that he deliberately picked.

And God supported him in that. In chapter 23 verse 11:

### Acts 23:11
> ¹¹But on the following night, the Lord stood near him and said, “Be courageous! For as you have testified to the *truth* about Me in Jerusalem, so you must testify in Rome also.”

### With Paul
So, whether Paul rebeled as some argue or not. We see that God was with Paul. He encourages Paul, and this words should encourage us as well. Even when you found yourself in such complicated situation, God is still is in control. Knowing the end of the story, we know that because of his imprisonment, Paul was able to testify, to witness, to share the gospel with several kings. With people of the highest rank in Roman empire. And in the end with the emperor of the Rome himself. Isn't that remarkable?

God was able to yeld some remarkble outcomes from this very intense, very difficult situation. He was in control. He is a winner. And as for Paul, he was not only ready, but happy to do those thinks. As we will shortly see.

### Back to Philippians 
But because from human point of view Paul's imprisonment looked like defeat. As if God's plan failed. As if something went wrong. People were concerned and worried. And rightfully so considering all the thing Paul went through.

That's why in his letter Paul tells Philippians not to be discouraged because of his imprisonment. We read "Joy" and "rejoice" many times through the letter. Which tells us Paul is not depressed about his situation. He embraced his position. And honestly he doesn't sound like he is desperate for relieve. In Philibians 1:19 Paul talks about deliverance. But, in verse 20 we see that he considers both options **LIFE** as well as **DEATH**. Both options are viable for Him.

### Philippians 1:18-21
> ...I also will rejoice,¹⁹for I know that this will turn out for my deliverance through your prayers and the provision of the Spirit of Jesus Christ,²⁰according to my eager expectation and hope, that I will not be put to shame in anything, but *that* with all boldness, Christ will even now, as always, be exalted in my body, whether by life or by death.


Unlike many of us, Paul was not afraid of death. When we get into circumstances, all we want is to get relieved. And most of the time it means to continue to live. And I think same was position of Philippines church. They wanted, Paul to continue to live. That's what their prayers were about. But Paul's attitude is a bit different. In the verse 21 he says:


### Philippians 1:21-30
> ²¹For to me, to live is Christ, and to die is gain.


He letting us know that he will be happy either way. Last time we read that that everybody knew that Paul is in chains *because of Christ*, or *for the sake of Christ*. Which means that everybody knew he's not some kind of a criminal. That Paul did nothing wrong and it was not some sort of a shady business on his side that cased him trouble. Everyone knew that **He is there for Christ.**

If you ever wanted to have same kind of boldness as Paul, today we will reveal his secret.

Paul had boldness in such complicated situation, because he knew that this is the place where God wants him to be... When we read verse 21 it sounds rather radical. But this verse is the key that let's us understand his Paul's assurance. Paul explains to Philippians and to us his attitude to life:

### Philippians 1:22-30
> ²²But if *I am* to live *on* in the flesh, this *will mean* fruitful labor for me; and I do not know which to choose.²³But I am hard-pressed from both *directions,* having the desire to depart and be with Christ, for *that* is very much better;²⁴yet to remain on in the flesh is more necessary for your sakes.²⁵Convinced of this, I know that I will remain and continue with you all for your progress and joy in the faith,²⁶so that your pride in Christ Jesus may be abundant because of me by my coming to you again.

Paul says that **to live In flesh means to continue his work for Christ**. He is not looking forward to some sort of retirement. When he would be able to travel around and see the world. When he would finally  leave for himself a little bit. No, he says that if he is to stay, he will continue with his ministry. And his only reasoning for life is his concern is other people. He doesn't talk about himself at all.

On the other hand, Paul says that "if I am to die", it is still very good option for me. In fact, he says if I would think about myself, I'd chose this one. He says it's very much better, or other translations say it's better by far.

Let's think about it. Church praying for Paul's relief, for his freedom. They don't want Paul to die. I think that even very possibility of it terrifies them. And I think this is proper attitude on their side.

But Paul tells them that actually I don't mind dying for Christ. He says if I would to chose. It that would be up to me to decide, I wold probably choose to depart and be with Christ.

Paul says that ether outcome is in his favor. 

This is Paul's secret of not being afraid of hardships and even death.
**Is to know that you are in God's will**. Is to be sure that you leave the life God wants you to leave. That you are right were God wants you to be. And you do what God wants you to do. If you will be sure of this things, you'll be able to have the same attitude Paul had.

**If your life is Christ, then to die is gain.**

How do we apply it to our current situation?
