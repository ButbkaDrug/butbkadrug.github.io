### Intro.
In our scripture for today, Paul continue to explain his situation to believers in Philippy. If you are new today and just as a reminder for everyone who maybe missed few Sundays. We are going through the letter to Philippians. Apostle Paul is in a Roman prison. He sends a letter to his friends to say thank you and encourage them.

Christians in Philippy loved Paul very much. He was an important figure in their lives. He started their church and brought them up.

They were very concerned about him and his situation. Because they knew that it can result in 2 ways. One way is what they desire and pray for:  Paul's release. But the other option is not so appealing and it is Paul's execution.

### Project to our life
Can you imagine something of that sort happening to our friend? To someone who is very dear to your heart?

I think you would also be very concerned. You would want this person to be freed, for all accusations to be dropped.

We would not spare our resources to help that person. To speak for them. Pray for them. Provide financial support etc.

And there is nothing wrong with that. I believe that this is proper attitude. You should feel this way. And react this way.

You know, if your friend is detained under false accusations. Potentially facing death sentence and it doesn't bother you at all... I mean, don't get me wrong, but I think something is wrong with you.

**It should bother you!** You should lose your sleep over it. It should occupy your mind.

And in a situations like that, we can start having questions: **Where is God?** **Why he's not intervene the situation?**

We can get discouraged in our faith if situation does not quickly resolve. It can be a challenge for our faith. Isn't it?

### Recent Situation
We have remotely similar situation just recently. Our dear brother got detained by immigration service. And end up in so called "immigration prison". Even though he did everything possible on his side to follow the law.

And you know what? **We were** concerned about him. It did not feel right. It didn't feel like God's will, at least to me it didn't.

Our brother was a good Christian. Faithful minister. He served the Lord here in Batumi. He ministered to unreached people group. Sharing gospel with them. And it was pretty successful. So, logically one would think that nothing like that shouldn't ever happen to him. Right?

### When it happens
And indeed when such thing happens, it almost immediately raises a question: **Where is God?** **Why he doesn't help his faithful minister?**

**Because obviously it cannot be God's will...**

Or can it?

If you familiar with such feelings, if such questions doesn't sound completely foreign to you. Our  today's scripture will shed some light on what might be happening behind the scenes. We will see whether it something that we should be afraid of? And what we should do in our current situation of evolving complications.

### History
And in order to understand Paul's situation better, we need to go back to the book of acts. We will look at the end of the third missionary journey of an Apostle Paul. At the point when things starts to evolve:

Let's open **Acts 19** and jump to the verses where Paul and his companions reached the goal of their journey. In Verse 21 Apostle Luke writes following:

### Acts 19:21
> ²¹Now after these things were finished, Paul resolved in the Spirit to go to Jerusalem after he had passed through Macedonia( which means through the church in Phillipy) and Achaia, saying, “After I have been there, I must also see Rome.”

### Personal conviction
In this verses, we can see that Paul and the team(and from now one I'll just say Paul, but keep in mind he was not alone) accomplished the goal of their third journey. And, you know, and naturally they came to a point when they need to decide what's next? This trip is finished. They did all they wanted to do. So, what do we do now?

Our text says "after this things were finished **Paul resolved in the Spirit**." Or as other translations say "**He felt compelled by the Spirit..." In other words we can say that  this idea came from Holy Spirit.

Idea to go to Jerusalem came from Holy Spirit. Or at least it was approved by the Holy Spirit.

Why it is important to understand?

Because it give us reassurance that Paul didn't stepped out of God's will. That trip to Jerusalem was not a mistake. That all the trouble Paul got into was not a result of his sin or anything else.

We can clearly see that in fact Paul followed the guidance of the Holy Spirit. That this was God's plan. And first confirmation of that Paul had **conviction in the Spirit**.

### The Prophet
And as they continue Towards Jerusalem, whose spirit keeps speaking about what will happen there. They are not going unaware. Rather opposite in act 20 verses 22 and 23 we read:

### Acts 20:22,23
²²And now, behold, bound by the Spirit, I am on my way to Jerusalem, not knowing what will happen to me there,²³except that the Holy Spirit solemnly testifies to me in every city, saying that chains and afflictions await me.

And later in chapter 21 they already reached Tyre Luke writes:
### Acts 21:4
⁴After looking up the disciples, we stayed there for seven days; and they kept telling Paul, through the Spirit, not to set foot in Jerusalem.


And at last when they got even closer to Jerusalem. We read about prophet coming from Judea in verses 10 and 11:

### Acts 21:10-11
> ¹⁰...a prophet named Agabus came down from Judea.¹¹And he came to us and took Paul’s belt and bound his own feet and hands, and said, “This is what the Holy Spirit says: ‘In this way the Jews in Jerusalem will bind the man who owns this belt and hand him over to the Gentiles.’

You see. God were speaking a lot about what would happen. I think it was important for both Paul and other people. Including his companions and believers in the churches they visit. At this point everybody new what is about to happen. Some people argue that Paul was in direct rebellion based on the verse 4 chapter 21. But, personally I don't think so. I tend to think that it was their interpretation of what is Holy Spirit saying rather then Paul's rebel. It's mostly irrelevant to our study for tonight. But we will try to look at both possibilities. 

I think that all this prophecies are so that me and you, as well as companions of Paul and believers would know that God is in control. At this point, it was very clear that Paul will get arrested. It was very clear that bad things will happen to Paul if he will step into the city.

And when people would hear this, they have typical, very normal, very human reaction. Let's continue reading in verse 12:

### Acts 21:12-14
> ”¹²When we had heard this, we as well as the local residents *began* begging him not to go up to Jerusalem.¹³Then Paul replied, “What are you doing, weeping and breaking my heart? For I am ready not only to be bound, but even to die in Jerusalem for the name of the Lord Jesus.”

> ¹⁴And since he would not be persuaded, we became quiet, remarking, “The will of the Lord be done!”

We can see that Paul have had a choice. He knew ahead of time what will happen. And no one would judge him if he would decide not to go. In fact, as we can see people bagged him not to go. Not only regular believers. But also apostle Luke and Timothy, his companion and some other mighty men of God, who have been through rough times together with Paul.

### Observation
So, we can clearly see that Paul's imprisonment was not some kind of mistake. Not an error or result of confusion. That even though we don't like to think this way, it was the will of God.

Another confirmation to this is in chapter 23 verse 11:

### Acts 23:11
> ¹¹But on the following night, the Lord stood near him and said, “Be courageous! For as you have testified to the *truth* about Me in Jerusalem, so you must testify in Rome also.”

### With Paul
God was with Paul. He encourages Paul, and this words encourage us, that even if you found yourself in such complicated situation, God is still is in control. Because of his imprisonment, Paul was able to testify, to witness, to share the gospel with several kings. And in the end with the emperor of Rome. Isn't that remarkable?

So, I don't think that Paul's imprisonment was a mistake on his side. He followed lead of Spirit. And He was not only ready, but happy to do those thinks. As we will shortly see.

### Back to Philippians 
But because from human point of view Paul's imprisonment looked like defeat. As if God's plan failed. As if something went wrong. People were concerned and worried. And rightfully so considering all the thing Paul went through.

That's why in his letter Paul tells Philippians not to be discouraged because of his imprisonment. We read "Joy" and "rejoice" many times through the letter. Which tells us Paul is not depressed about his situation. He embraced his position. And honestly he doesn't sound like he is desperate for relieve. In Philibians 1:19 Paul talks about deliverance. But, in verse 20 we see that he considers both options **LIFE** as well as **DEATH**. Both options are viable for Him.

### Philippians 1:18-21
> ...I also will rejoice,¹⁹for I know that this will turn out for my deliverance through your prayers and the provision of the Spirit of Jesus Christ,²⁰according to my eager expectation and hope, that I will not be put to shame in anything, but *that* with all boldness, Christ will even now, as always, be exalted in my body, whether by life or by death.


Unlike many of us, Paul was not afraid of death. When we get into circumstances, all we want is to get relieved. And most of the time it means to continue to live. And I think same was position of Philippines church. They wanted, Paul to continue to live. That's what their prayers were about. But Paul's attitude is a bit different. In the verse 21 he says:


### Philippians 1:21-30
> ²¹For to me, to live is Christ, and to die is gain.


He letting us know that he will be happy either way. Last time we read that that everybody knew that Paul is in chains *because of Christ*, or *for the sake of Christ*. Which means that everybody knew he's not some kind of a criminal. That Paul did nothing wrong and it was not some sort of a shady business on his side that cased him trouble. Everyone knew that **He is there for Christ.**

If you ever wanted to have same kind of boldness as Paul, today we will reveal his secret.

Paul had boldness in such complicated situation, because he knew that this is the place where God wants him to be... When we read verse 21 it sounds rather radical. But this verse is the key that let's us understand his Paul's assurance. Paul explains to Philippians and to us his attitude to life:

### Philippians 1:22-30
> ²²But if *I am* to live *on* in the flesh, this *will mean* fruitful labor for me; and I do not know which to choose.²³But I am hard-pressed from both *directions,* having the desire to depart and be with Christ, for *that* is very much better;²⁴yet to remain on in the flesh is more necessary for your sakes.²⁵Convinced of this, I know that I will remain and continue with you all for your progress and joy in the faith,²⁶so that your pride in Christ Jesus may be abundant because of me by my coming to you again.

Paul says that **to live In flesh means to continue his work for Christ**. He is not looking forward to some sort of retirement. When he would be able to travel around and see the world. When he would finally  leave for himself a little bit. No, he says that if he is to stay, he will continue with his ministry. And his only reasoning for life is his concern is other people. He doesn't talk about himself at all.

On the other hand, Paul says that "if I am to die", it is still very good option for me. In fact, he says if I would think about myself, I'd chose this one. He says it's very much better, or other translations say it's better by far.

Let's think about it. Church praying for Paul's relief, for his freedom. They don't want Paul to die. I think that even very possibility of it terrifies them. And I think this is proper attitude on their side.

But Paul tells them that actually I don't mind dying for Christ. He says if I would to chose. It that would be up to me to decide, I wold probably choose to depart and be with Christ.

Paul says that ether outcome is in his favor. 

This is Paul's secret of not being afraid of hardships and even death.
**Is to know that you are in God's will**. Is to be sure that you leave the life God wants you to leave. That you are right were God wants you to be. And you do what God wants you to do. If you will be sure of this things, you'll be able to have the same attitude Paul had.

**If your life is Christ, then to die is gain.**

How do we apply it to our current situation?
