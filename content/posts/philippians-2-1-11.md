### Paul's idea
That is what we will attempt to uncover today. And great tool that will help us to understand this peace of scripture is *lexical analysis*... We will look into an original text, and as we do so we would find out that the same word keeps coming up - "**φρονέω**" [phroneō]. Paul keeps repeating it again and again. Which typically means we should pay our attention!

### φρονέω
So, what is this word? This is one of those words that is not that easy to translate. When you get to a point of sufficient understanding of the second language. You start to realize that sometimes, you cannot easily translate one word of the language to the other language. 

Sometimes there is just no equivalent word for that. Sometimes the word is so tightly integrated into the culture, that you would need entire paragraph to explain the meaning. And even then you will probably fail to give comprehensive explanation. And our word is one of those...

**Phroneo** - is about how you think... What you value... The mindset... All those little believes and experiences, that shape your actions.  But also, *proneo* is a deliberate choice. It is decision you make after reasoning...

...

### Jesus's mindset
In the verse 5 Paul writes:

### Philippians 2:5(NLT)
> ⁵You must have the "same attitude" that Christ Jesus had.

What is this "same attitude?" Again, he uses word *phroneō*. He talks about our conchies choice to act in a certain way. In other words Paul says: "Look at Jesus. And copy His attitude. His model of behaviour. Reason about life in a same way he did"

### Philippians 2:6-8(NLT)
 ⁶Though he was God, he did not think of equality with God as something to cling to.⁷Instead, he gave up his divine privileges; he took the humble position of a slave and was born as a human being. When he appeared in human form,⁸he humbled himself in obedience to God and died a criminal’s death on a cross.


### What do we see there?
What do we learn about Jesus in this passage?

Jesus already was God. He was at the top. At the best position possible.  It's really cant get any better then this. He was at the highest position, at the best place possible. But he didn't cling, he didn't held on to his privileges. And when it came to it. He gave up his divine privileges in order to save us.

Verse 7 says *he took the position of a slave...*. He left that beautiful place. And put on a far less superior form of a human... He became a slave.

And if we will flip a page back in our bible and look into chapter 1 verse 1. In the same way Paul and Timothy talk about themselves:

### Philippians 1:1
> ¹This letter is from Paul and Timothy, **slaves** of Christ Jesus...

Paul adopted the same *phroneo*, way of reasoning as Jesus. When Paul came to faith, he also had to give up his privileged position, his high status. Career... He gave up everything. And became a slave...

Which is opposite attitude to what we find in verse 3:

### Philippians 2:3(NASB)
³Do nothing from **selfishness** or **empty conceit**...

I was struggling to translate the "empty conceit" last week so I looked it up. And basically it is self centered ambition. It is when you do thing for appearance in recognition. When you get the honor you do not deserve...

Just like some people did in philippians 1:17

### Philippians 1:17
¹⁷Those others do not have pure motives as they preach about Christ. They preach with selfish ambition, not sincerely, intending to make my chains more painful to me.

### Ambitions and Pride
 I don't think that this instruction only apply to our "church life". Even though often we do, maybe even subconchensly, draw the line between: *this is how I behave in church* or *among believers*. And *this is how I behave outside, in the world*. Somehow we have this dual standards. And same truth does not apply to all areas of our life. And it is not right. It is however the topic of the other day.

But as we can see. Even when you do, what most people would consider "God's work". It is still very much possible to do it out of your own ambition and pride. It was true 2000 years ago, it is still true today. 

I hope it is not the case in our fellowship. But, people do treat living for the Lord as a *corporate ladder*. And instead of having the mindset of Jesus. Instead of reasoning the way He did. They take mindset of the world and bring it to the church\ministry. And start to treat life for the Lord as yet another opportunity to presue their own goals and interests. Promoting themselves, utilizing their position and status.

But it is not what Jesus did:

### Philippians 2:7,8
⁷Instead, he gave up his divine privileges; he took the humble position of a slave and was born as a human being. When he appeared in human form,⁸he humbled himself in obedience to God and died a criminal’s death on a cross.

Jesus could have ambitions. He could overthrow Romans. He could overthrow rotten religious system. He could become the **The King**. In fact disciples bagged him to do exactly that. To take advantage of His power.

But Jesus says: "Hey, I came here to serve, and to love... Not to rule..." Life of Jesus was not about self fulfillment. It was about doing what our Father in heaven wanted Him to do.

And as the citizens of his kingdom we should adapt this mindset. 

**I'm here to serve, not to rule**

### Matthew 20:25-28
²⁵But Jesus called them together and said, “You know that the rulers in this world lord it over their people, and officials flaunt their authority over those under them.²⁶But among you it will be different. Whoever wants to be a leader among you must be your servant,²⁷and whoever wants to be first among you must become your slave.²⁸For even the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve others and to give his life as a ransom for many.”

Our EGO will get in a way... That's why we need to make a choice. We need to adopt our Lord's *Phroneo* - the way of reasoning about what is important. 

### Result of the obedience
And it's not like we loose something doing that. In the following verse we can see what was the outcome of such mindset:

### Philippians 2:9-11
 ⁹Therefore, God elevated him to the place of highest honor and gave him the name above all other names,¹⁰that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth,¹¹and every tongue declare that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

Obedience to the Father brought Jesus so much more then he could ever get, pursuing his own ambition in the wordily manner. He won by loosing...

### For Us
When we set aside our ambition and decide to adopt "phroneo" of Jesus. **"To serve, and not to rule..."**
When we stop seeking empty glory. Give up our position, privileges and etc. For the sake of being obedient to our Father. God will give us so much more then we could ever have ourselves. 

We might temporally loose some perishable riches, some *empty* glory. But, as a reword we will get incomparably more. That's the beautiful part of being in the kingdom of God. You can't really loose. There is no inflation. It is not like trading, when you can burn your life savings in a flash.

When we invest into kingdom of God, we receive at least x100 back.

But in order to enjoy the benefits of God's kingdom. We need to live like it's citizens. We should genuinely change our *phroneo*. The way we reason about life. And switch to a new model. And as I already mention it requires effort.

### Philippians 2:12,13
 ¹²Dear friends, you always followed my instructions when I was with you. And now that I am away, it is even more important. Work hard to show the results of your salvation, obeying God with deep reverence and fear.¹³For God is working in you, giving you the desire and the power to do what pleases him.

### Conclusion
It will take an effort on our side. God will never **take over your will**. You need to do this choice. But as we live like a citizens of heaven, God himself will work in us and through us. Giving us directions, desire and all the things necessary, helping us to accomplish His work.
