### PRESENCE vs ABSENCE
> ...just as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling

From this text we see that at the very least Philippians were good in obedience so far. Paul, says "*as you have always obeyed*" in my presence. So, at least for as long as Paul was around, they were doing pretty well in living their lives in Godly way.

I thik we can all agree that it is easier to stick to something while your mentor, the one you respect is around.

### Story about construction

Like kids when parats are home. They typically behave better. You might have to go through never ending battle every night by making them shower and brush their teeth. But at least they do that. They act if that's what YOU need. Not realizing that this is what THEY need.

But if a kid does not behave in the presents of his parants. Aint no way he gonna behave better in their absense.

Bible teach this principle. If you not able to handle small responsibility. You will definetely not gonna handle larger one. It's kinda obvious, right?

In Luke 16 Jesus says:

### Luke 16:10-13

### Philippians 2:12-14
> ...but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling...

Paul is saying you learned how to live for God. And you successfuly did that together with me. Don't stop now, when I'm gone. While Paul was among them he would help them to stay on track. Encorage and correct. But not once he is gone. Naturally the attantion level would drop. The pashion would cool down a little. Like a fist gear in the car, it give you nice bump in the beginning. And that's what Paul encorages them to do. WORK OUT your selvation. Continue to live out those relationships with God you have obtained. Continue to live like a sitizents of heaven. Continue to walk in HUMILITY that expressed in OBEDIENCE.
