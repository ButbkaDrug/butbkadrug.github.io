### UNITY
Few things which we have covered are:

* Following Christ is a **team sport**?

Remember we talked that being the church is not like a *book lovers club*. Which meets couple times a week to discuss their favorite parts of the story. Exchange some opinions and maybe to argue a little bit.

But that the church more closely resembels sports team. Like rackby or football. Or like anchient Roman military formation. Where solders fight sholder to shulder. Codepended on one another. Having to stand for one another and cover each others back.

Kind of a situation when you do not really do "solo". It's a **TOGETHER** type of activity.

Apostle Paul in the chapter one throwing some military/sports terms at us. STEKO and ***(fill in later). To make sure that the point "*hit's home*" so to speak. Because some portion of Philippian church had a military background, so it sounded extremely close and relevant to their heats.

All in all **UNITY** is the key point. **Unity** that assumes deep care for one another. And not just any type of unity. But the type of unity you find in a healthy family. When it's not just about "busness". But, where people genuanely conserned about one another. When people are actually preset and caring. Even if it takes away from them.

### Philippians 2:4
> ⁴do not *merely* look out for your own personal *interests,* but also for the *interests* of others.

So, **TOGETHERNESS** and **UNITY** are the must for the healthy fellowship.

### HUMILITY
The second strong point we covered was that in order to have such level of Unity, we would have to **humble ourselves**. This exact attutue of our Lord Jesus should be in us:

### Philippians 2:5
⁵Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus...

Following verses tell us that Jesus **HUMBLED** himself to extreme degree which was expressed in extreme **OBIDIENT** to the Heavenly Father. And for that reason God **EXOLTED** Him and **BESTOWED** on Him ... all the things.

### KEYS
So, if we are looking to be the kind of Church, that pleases God. If we are looking to server God then - **HUMILITY** which expressed through **OBIDIENCE** is the way to go. This is the way of Kingdom of God. Otherwise it's not gonna work.

### PRESENCE vs ABSENCE
> ...just as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling

From this text we see that Philippians were doing well so far. Paul, says "*as you have always obeyed*" in my presence.... So, at least for as long as Paul was around, they were succeeding in their walk with the Lord.

But now, as Paul writes his letter, comes that pivital moment in their jurney when they have to continue on their own.

The key person, who started their ghurch. Who helped them to grow. Who taught them everyghing they know. This person is no longer with them and very well might never be coming back.

When you just start your jurney with God. Often you have a key figure in your life. Maybe it's a frined or church leader, maybe pastor. The one you look up to. The one that serves as an example. And it's not a bed thing. It can motivate you a lot, to overcome your shortcomings and grow, and mature. 

### Story
When me and my wife started to serve in mission full time. It was in Armenia many years ago. We had a good leader. And I looked up to him. And I learned from him. Learned from his teaching but even more so learned by being around. By doing things together.

And when sometimes our leader would talk about leaving his position and going to serve somewhere else. I would think to myself that in such case I probobly would not stay there ether. You see, at that stage. Without even realizing it, I shared IN HIS passion, IN HIS work, IN HIS vision.

But one day it had to change. We were forced to move out of Armenia. And we came to the pivital moment in our ministry. When we were "on our own", so to speak. I did not have someone elese faith, passion and vision to share in. We had to **work out our own** selvation.

### What about you?
You can be at the stage in your life where you share someone elses pashion for the Lord. When it's not your own yet. And it not nesseserly wrong. This is how you grow. But, sooner or later comes the moment when you can't live by someonelses passion. When you have to learn to **work out your own** selvation. To be obidient in the absence.

### Philippians 2:12-14
> ...but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling...

There is already number of people who moved to Batumi from their churches and dissapeared. I talk to Pastors when I'm visiting home and they would be like: "Do you know this guy?", I'm like "No... Neve seen him". And they are like strange, they moved to Batumi 3, 6, 12 month ago. They never missed one sayday at home...

So many students come here for study. Never missed one Sunday at home, but rarly or never come to church here.

> "Not only in my presentes, but even more in my absense..." Paul.

We should take ownership over our faith, over our walk with the Lord. And learn to be obidient not only in the precente, but also in the absense of our key people. We got to find our own passion, our own vision, our own love for God. When we will continue to **work out** our selvation. Live by the principles of the kingdom. Not because this is what "church" tells you to do. But because you love God. Because you understand that it is best for you.

Like kids who does not want to brush their teeth. And you litteraly have to have entire arguments with them to persue them to go and actually brush their teeth. For a while they do it fo YOU. For those of you who don't have kids. I'll tell you a secret. You can absolute ruin someones day just by asking "Have you brushed your teeth?". 

But once they mature a little. They start to realize that, it's not for ME they brashing their teeth. But, for their own benefit. Because otherwise things go south... And you don't have to check on them anymore. They start to do this on their own. They take the ownership over their health.

Same with our walk with the Lord. At one time you come to church, your read your bible, you pray because your mom told you so. Or because otherwise people be asking where have you've been. Or milion of other reasons.

You force changes in your life motivetad by others. But in reality this is God working in you.

### Philippians 2:13
¹³for it is God who is at work in you, both to desire and to work for *His* good pleasure.

So, even if your key person have a failure of faith. Falls into sin, leaves the church. It doesn't mean it all waisted now. You need to continue to **work out**  on your own now. Yes, it's harder. But, God healps you. He is doing the work. Producing decire and giving you energy to fulfill those good desires.

### Conclusion
God value obedience extremely highly.

### 1 Samuel 15:22
“Does the LORD have as much delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices As in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than a sacrifice, *And* to pay attention is *better* than the fat of rams.

Your obidience to the Lord means to hey way more then anything you can do "for Him". We read the same in our today scripture too. And whole bible full of it. It repeated again and again.

First we have to learn obidience. In the family, in the church. We learn by watching our key people. How they obey and foolow the Lord. We learn to **be obidient in the presence**.

It require **HUMILITY**. Another characteristic that God values extremely highly.

**Showing obedience in the presentce**, in your family, in your church - is your playground. If you fail to be obedient, to exercise humility there. Then aint no way you will be able to obey God outside of that setting.

There is considerable part of the people who preffer the state of a VISITOR. Because, they are trying to avoid this very thing. Accountability and Authority.

That's why people gladly join so called "online" churches. Because there is no accountability, there is no authority over you. You don't have to exercise humility and obedience if you don't want to. No one knows you and your life really. Because it's all just online.

But guess what? It is God who work in you. Through your familiy, thourhg your church. God forbid though a pastor...

And if you failed even at that stage. If you failed to be obidient **IN THE PRESENTCE** You not gonna succeed in obedientce in the absence.
