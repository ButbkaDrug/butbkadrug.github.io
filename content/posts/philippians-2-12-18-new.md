### UNITY
Few things which we have covered so far:

* Following Christ is a **team sport**?

Remember we talked that being the church is not like a *book lover's club*. Which meets couple times a week to discuss their favorite parts of the story. Exchange some opinions and maybe to argue a little bit.

But that church more closely resembles a sports team. Like rackby or football. Or maybe like ancient Roman military formation. Where solders fight shoulder to shoulder. Codependent on one another. Having to stand for one another and cover each others back. United with one goal. Having one intent. United in mind and spirit for the sake of the common goal.

It is a Kind of situation when you do not really do "solo". It's a **TOGETHER** type of activity.

Apostle Paul in the chapter one throws some military/sports terms at us. To make sure that point "*hit's home*" so to speak. It might not be the most relevant analogy for us. Because not so many have a military background. But good portion of Philippian church on another hand. May very well come from a military background, so it would sound extremely close and relevant to their heats.

All in all **UNITY** is the key point. **Unity** that assumes deep care for one another. And not just any type of unity. But the type of unity you find in a healthy family. When it's not just about "business". But, it's where people genuinely concerned about one another. It's when people are actually preset and caring. Even if it takes away from them.

### Philippians 2:4
> ⁴do not *merely* look out for your own personal *interests,* but also for the *interests* of others.

So, **TOGETHERNESS** and **UNITY** are **the must** for the healthy fellowship.

### HUMILITY
The second strong point we covered was that in order to have Unity, we would have to **humble ourselves**. This exact attitude, characteristic of our Lord Jesus should be present also in us:

### Philippians 2:5
⁵Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus...

Following verses tell us that Jesus **HUMBLED** himself to extreme degree which was expressed in extreme **OBEDIENCE** to the Heavenly Father. And for that reason God **EXALTED** Him and **BESTOWED** on Him ... all the things you can read about in verses 5-11.

### KEYS
So, if we are looking to be the kind of Church, that pleases God. If we are looking to **server God**, then HUMILITY** which expressed through **OBEDIENCE** is the way to go. This is the way of Kingdom of God. Otherwise it's not gonna work.

### SO THEN
Paul starts this section of the letter from saying "*so then*". Or, other way to translate that would be **considering**.

Considering that **HUMILITY** of Jesus, which was expressed in **OBEDIENCE** is the true way to please God. Considering that it is the way of the Kingdom, it is the way to get eternal reward. Considering all that the next logical second step is to start applying it to our own lives.

Understanding - is only the beginning. Practicing it's where it all at. Because, we all can talk about humility all day long. But, there is great chasm between **talking about** it and **exercising it**. Isn't it?

I can be an expert on growing muscles. And indeed I know quite a few things about it. But, you don't see very much muscle on me. Because, I don't exercise that knowledge. Therefore it does not benefit me very much. But on the other hand our gym bros. They might don't talk about exercising much, but without a doubt you know they exercise. Just by looking at em.

So, It's not about knowing - it's about exercising.

Paul continues: 

### PRESENCE vs ABSENCE
> ...just as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling

From this text we see that Philippians were doing well so far. Paul, says "*as you have always obeyed*" in my presence.... So, at least for as long as Paul was around, they were succeeding in their walk with the Lord.

But now, as Paul writes his letter, they come to that pivotal moment in their journey, when they would have to continue on their own.

The key person, who started their church. Who helped them to grow. Who taught them everything they know. This person is no longer with them and very well might never be coming back.

### It's ok for the start
When you just start your journey with God. Often you have a key figure in your life. Maybe it's a friend who brought you to church. Maybe a church leader, maybe even a pastor. It doesn't really matter. The one you look up to. The one that serves as an example. It's just haw it is. And it's not a bed thing. This person's example can motivate you a lot, to overcome your shortcomings and grow, and mature. 

### Story
When me and my wife started to serve in mission full time. It was in Armenia many years ago. We were blessed to have a good mission leader. And I looked up to him. And I learned from him. Learned from his teaching yes, but even more so I learned by being around him. By doing things together.

And when sometimes our leader would talk about leaving his position and going to serve somewhere else. I would think to myself that in such case I probably would not stay in Armenia ether. You see, at that stage. Without even realizing it, I shared **IN HIS** passion, **IN HIS** work, IN **HIS vision**.

But one day it had to change. We were forced to move out of Armenia. And we came to that pivotal moment in our ministry. When we are "on our own", so to speak. I did not have someone else's faith, passion and vision to share in. We had to **work out OUR OWN** salvation.

We learned to be obedient **in the presence** of our key figure. Who motivated us. Supported and encored us with his example. Who were more mature and experienced. But, time has come to learn to be obedient **in the absence**.

### What about you?
You can be at the stage in your life where you share someone else's passion for the Lord. When it's not your own yet. And it not nesseserly wrong. This is the way to grow. But, sooner or later comes the moment when you can't live by someone else's passion anymore. When you have to learn to **work out your own** salvation. To be obedient in the absence. Just as Paul says:

### Philippians 2:12-14
> ...but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling...

There are already a number of people who moved to Batumi from their churches and disappeared. I talk to Pastors when I'm visiting home and they would be like: "Do you know this guy? Does he comes to your church?", I'm like "No... I have never seen him". And they are like strange, he moved to Batumi 3, 6, 12 month ago. They never missed one Sunday at home...

So many students come here for study. Never missed one Sunday at their home church, but rarely or never come to church here.

So it's important to be able to be obedient:
> "Not only in my presents, but even more in my ab sense..." Paul.

### Ownership
We should take ownership over our faith, over our walk with the Lord. And learn to be obedient not only in the presence, but also in the absence of our key people. Outside of the church setting. We got to find our own passion, our own vision, our own love for God.

The kind of love that will allow us to continue to work out our salvation - to Live by the principles of the kingdom of God. Not because this is what “church” tells you to do. But because you love God.

And also do not cease desires to do things for God. Because those most likely come from Him.

### Philippians 2:13
> ¹³for it is God who is at work in you, both to desire and to work for His good pleasure.

So, even if your key person leaves. Moves to another country or something. Maybe even has a failure of faith. Falls into sin, heresy or leaves the church. It doesn’t mean it all wasted. That you should leave too.

No, continue to work out your own salvation. Yes, it’s harder. But, God will continue to help. Because it was him all the way, producing desire to do good. And giving you energy to fulfill those good desires.


### Conclusion
Wrapping up we can definitely say that God value obedience extremely highly:

1 Samuel 15:22
“Does the LORD have as much delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices As in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than a sacrifice, And to pay attention is better than the fat of rams.

Obedience is way more important than anything you can do “for God”. 

We read the same in our scripture for toady as well. Bible repeats this truth again and again.

We learn to be obedient to God. By exercising obedience in our family also in the church. It really helps if we see our key people walk in obedience. This is how we learn to be **obedient in the presence**.

It requires **HUMILITY**. Another characteristic that God values extremely highly.

Showing obedience in the presence, in your family, in your church is like a playground. It is the place to learn and to grow. But if you fail to be obedient, to exercise humility there. Then no way you will be able to obey God in more challenging situations outside of that setting.

If you failed to be obedient **IN THE PRESENCE** You not gonna succeed in **obedience in the absence**.
