### Intro
Greetings dear brothers and sisters, we continue to go over the letter to the church in Philipy. Few words to remind all of us the context. In order To make sure that we all "on the same page" so to speak.

### Context
Unlike other letters of Paul, letter to this church does not address any particular issue. It's like those people were not perfect, but all in all it seems to be pretty healthy and mature fellowship of believers. They've been Paul's partner's for many years. And have also supported Paul in his imprisonment, by sending him some financial help as well as one of their member to serve apostle in his needs. Because, even though Paul were allowed not to be in the dungeon. He were still under arrest. Which makes your life hard. But, that for the next times.

### Current
Currently we're at the middle of the chapter two. In the verse 12. We will continue to explore the topic started at the end of the chapter one. Few weeks ago we started to talk about **LIVING AS CITIZENS OF HEAVEN**. Or **living the life worthy of the Gospel**.

### What was covered so far.
Which means a few things. One of the first things we see is that it's a **team sport**. Not and individual activity. In verse 27 bible says:

### Philippians 1:27
> ²⁷ ...I will hear about you that you are standing firm[steko στήκω](Like soldier holding his position under attack) in one spirit, with one mind striving together[synathleō συναθλέω](Team athletes competing together in the same contest or To fight shoulder to shoulder, **Holding formation while advancing or resisting an enemy**) for the faith of the gospel;

 ### Together
 Paul uses some military language here. Because it is very likely that good portion of the Philippians church were ex militaries. And they well understood what is Paul trying to say. Think about roman formations. There were quite literally fighting **shoulder to shoulder**. They were in it together. Life of each solder depended on those around him. And each of them had to not only watch for themselves, but also to cover each other backs.

And this kind of mentality healthy church should adopt. This is what Paul means in the verse 4 of the chapter 2.

### Philippians 2:4
> ⁴do not *merely* look out for your own personal *interests,* but also for the *interests* of others.

We should remember that unlike our mostly egocentric society. As a followers of Christ we do not put ourselves into the center around which revolves entire universe. We have to look out for others as well. It's not about "what I want". But, about fulfilling the will of our Father, **together**.

### Humility
Which will require some humility on our part. We not gonna re read those verses, but Paul brings Jesus as an Ultimate example of humility:

Jesus humbles himself by taking the form of a man. Then when he was a man, he further humbled himself by becoming obedient[attentively listening] - to the point of death.

And in the verse 5 of the chapter two Paul say:

### Philippians 2:5
> ⁵Have this attitude in yourselves which was also in Christ Jesus,

And the key point he makes, the most important thing. I want you to note that, it might rub you the wrong way. But the most important thing  Is not striving for the power Jesus had. Not the miracles he performed. Those are cool side effects. But not the goal.

The most important is strive for His level of **obedience** and **humility**. Those two are the keys.

To be **humble** and **obedient**. And to obey - means [TO ATTENTIVELY LISTEN]. TO GIVE EAR. Listen and pay attention to whom? To what? To ATTENTIVELY LISTEN to what our Heavenly Father says.

### Philippians 2:12-14
> ¹²So then, my beloved, just as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling;¹³for it is God who is at work in you, both to desire and to work for *His* good pleasure.

### SO THEN
Paul starts this section of the letter from "so then". Which hits to us that, the following will be logical conclusion of what was said previously. And if you will look up couple verses you will see that Paul talks about Jesus.

That he 1 Humbled himself, then humbled himself even more, and then was **OBEDIENT** to extreme, to **THE END**. And as a result of **that**, this is why, God highly exalted Him. And he lists all the beautiful blessings are reward that were bestowed on Jesus for his **HUMILITY** and **OBEDIENCE**.

This two are the core characteristics of a true **CITIZEN OF HEAVEN**. You can't tell citizens of heaven by the color of their skin. You not gonna tell them by the shape of their eyes. Nor by their fitness. But this two, HUMILITY and OBEDIENCE are very strong indicators.

And if God values this characteristics so much, it only makes sense to adopt them, practice them, master them. And that's what effectivley Paul saying next.

### PRESENCE vs ABSENCE

> ...just as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling

That's a mouthful sentence. Let's try to break it down. 

### DO NOT STOP
Paul is saying do not stop pursuing relationships with God. You would continue to ACTIVELY LISTEN/GIVE EAR to what is God saying to you. He says you've been obedient when I was with you, it's even more important now, when I'm away.

Unfortunately in reality is often hard to do. It's easier to do the right thing when your leader/mentor/pastor is near. And require fare bit of maturity to continue to be the same way when you on your own.
