### Timothy
First person we read about is [[Timothy]]. I think most of us know who is the Timothy. But for those who don't I'll quickly go over his story without excessive details.

[[Timothy]] was a yang believer from the town of [[Lystra]]. It is in Lystra people though that Paul and Barnabas are Zeus and Hermes after the healing of the disabled man. Remember, apostles barely convinced them not to offer them sacrifices. But few days later very same people stoned Paul and dragged his body outside the city thinking he was dead. That's how Lystra treated Paul on his first visit.

Approximately year later [[Paul]] visits Lystra again. During his second missionary journey. He recruits a new team at the moment and invites Timothy(Acts 16:1) to join them. Bible says that Timothy was spoken well of by the brothers from [[Lystra]] and [[Iconium]](city near by). He was a quite yang. Probably in late teens or early 20s.

During the same year they come to [[Philippi]] and start a church there. Timothy stayed with Paul since then till the end(almost). Paul would send him to the churches to help them with their issues. To Thessalonica and Corinth and other churches in Macedonia including Philippi.

He was with Paul on his way to Jerusalem, when Paul got arrested. And accompanied Paul on his way to Rome too. Though the shipwreck and all that they've had to go through. He have stayed with Paul during his imprisonment in Rome as well. Later Timothy becomes an overseer of the Ephesian church. But that's outside the scope of our today's study

All in all Timothy went through a lot with Paul. At the point of writing Philipians chapter 2, which we continue to study today, they serve together over a decade. Timothy is in his 30's Paul is his 60's. So, he was tried and true companion. Paul knew him very well and Philippians knew him very well.

### Back to the text
As we go back to the text, as always there are several important and interesting points that we should look at. And as always we not gonna be able to cover them all. So, today I want us to concentrate on the concept of **PROVEN CHARACTER** mentioned in the verse 22. Paul says:

> "You know of his(Timothy's) PROVEN CHARACTER..."

### Quick Update
For all of you who are new, here is the situation. [[Paul]] is in Rome awaiting for his court case come before Cesar. [[Epaphroditus]] member of the Philippians church, comes to serve Paul in his needs and deliver some financial support. Along with that he also brings an update on the situation in the church.

Paul receives some worrisome news about fellowship in Philippi from Epaphroditus. But he cannot go and help them to figure it out. He needs someone qualified and trustworthy person. Who will go on his behalf and check on them. Who will help them to resolve their issues and get back on track if needed and bring the updates back to Paul.

### Best Candidate
The best candidate, at least within the reach is [[Timothy]]. There are some somethings about him, that set Timothy apart. Some things that make him to stand out among "everyone else".

**Here there are key characteristics** that we find in this scripture are:

    * Pall calls him **one of kindred spirit** or literally **equal in soul**. Meaning that Timothy shares Paul's vision and doctrin, love and burden for this fellowship. Timothy is genuanelly concerned of their well being.
    
        - As I mentioned before Timothy was not stranger in Philippi. He knew this church from the day 1. He helped to establish it and helped it grow. He head a heart for them.

    * He is ready to secrifice his own interests for the sake of serving this church. Unlike others who, as Paul says:  *seek after their own interests*.

        - Paul is very sure and assuring the fellowship, that Timothy does not have his own agenda. Just as much as himself. That Timothy cares only for the agenda of his Master, Lord Jesus Christ . And therefore only wanting the best for the fellowship.

    * He summorises his recomendation letter saying **You know of his PROVEN CHARACTER**. Meaning that Paul actually have no need to give him recomendations because church themselves know that Timothy is trustworthy. That he have right character for the job. Ha have proved himself in the past already and very much quiolified for the job.

When I first read this phrase **PROVEN CHARACTER**. I liked it a lot. I wanted to look it up. Especially since recently we've been talking a lot about importance and the role of christian or CHRIST LIKE character. 

So, when Paul say **PROVEN CHARACTER** It is something new? Can I find more about it? 

...

And guess what, it is not new at all. Actually we already encounter this term before. Even though you probably don't remember. As I didn't. Paul introduced the idea for PROVEN CHARACTER in his letter to Romans. On a New Testament timeline it was 10 years ago:

### Romans 5:3,4
> ³...but we also celebrate(glory, rejoice) in our **tribulations**, knowing that tribulation brings about perseverance; ⁴and perseverance, **proven character**; and **proven character**, hope;

Interesting... We not gonna re-study this passage from Romans today. But let's note that apparently **PROVEN CHARACTER** yields from **PERSEVERANCE**. Which comes by going through **TRIBULATIONS**! That's cannot be true... People on the internet say real Christians do not experience tribulations. Tribulation only for sinners...

Apparently not. Even an Ancient Roman stoic philosopher Seneca(the Younger) said:

> "Fire is the test of gold; adversity, of strong men."

...

Once again Paul throws shade at "prosperity gospel" lovers. Because not only bible says that we should embrace tribulations. It does not stop there, it says that we should also **CELEBRATE** tribulations! It's a Crazy Idea for the modern Christian world! But only during the time of tribulations you have opportunity to build and to prove your character!

### Back to Timothy
So, when we go back to Timothy we can see that what set's him apart is his **character**. Not anything else. And if you really think about it, that's something God continue to work on,  in every one of us, to the rest of our lives.

He wants us to continue to build CHRIST LIKE CHARACTER. And just like muscles, it's not gonna build itself. On our side we should exercise, we should work out. And the  **Lord** will bring growth.

Little fun fact for you. In Romans 5:3 Paul literally say that tribulations ***WORK OUT*** our PERSEVERANCE. 

So in Timothy we find an example of CHRIST LIKE or PROVEN CHARACTER. But he is not only one. 

### Epaphroditus
There is another example in our scripture for today:

### Philippians 2:25-30
²⁵~~But~~ I thought it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother and fellow worker and fellow soldier, who is also your messenger and minister to my need,²⁶because he was longing for you all and was distressed because you had heard that he was sick.²⁷For indeed he was sick to the point of death, but God had mercy on him, and not only on him but also on me, so that I would not have sorrow upon sorrow.²⁸Therefore I have sent him all the more eagerly, so that when you see him again you may rejoice and I may be less concerned *about you.*²⁹Receive him then in the Lord with all joy, and hold people like him in high regard,³⁰because he came close to death for the work of Christ, risking his life to compensate for your absence in your service to me.

Next person we read about is Epaphroditus. He was a member of the church in Philippi. Who were chosen by them to firstly deliver finances they gathered for Paul. And second to stay with Paul and minister to his needs. Because remember, Paul is under house arrest. He can't go anywhere. He can't do anything himself. He locked up. He have to pay rent. He have to buy food. Communicate with people. All that. There were no GLOVO or WALT who would deliver goods to you at the time. There were no internet. So, Epaphroditus were appointed to do just that take care of Paul.

To me it shows just how much Philippians cared for Paul. But, that's the topic for the other day. Today we looking at the character. What does Paul says about Epaphroditus:

Paul talks about him extremely highly. 

* He calls him **Brother** - meaning that they share the same faith in Jesus Christ.

* He calls him **fellow worker** - because they share in the same labor, same mission. In the work of the Gospel.

* And lastly he calls him **fellow solder** - because they also shared the same struggle same tribulations. Together, shoulder to shoulder. Just as we talked.

He also recognizes him as messenger and minister - who brought the news about the fellowship and served to Paul needs.

### Path from to Rome
We don't really underhand how big of a deal was to come from Philippi to Rome. I checked it out. Even now it is 1200 km or over 800 miles of walking. Often through the mountains. Exposed to a Sun and Rain and everything in between. Always under the threat of being robbed. There were no banks, you have to carry everything in cash. And everybody know that! How would you feel about waking with 30 grands in your backpack around a rough neighborhood? Not very fun, isn't it?

How about 7 weeks straight? Because that's how long it takes to complete this route. It can take up to 90 days. If you go in Summer. In winter maybe twice more because you can't use ships.

If you get sick. You on your own. If you getting robed, you on your own. It's not a simple task by any means. And Epaphroditus did get sick. Paul says: Almost died. Who knows what else he had to endure during the journey.

He literally walked from Batumi to Istanbul, and not along the highway thought the cities. No, though the mountains, though wilderness. And even that does not accurately portray the intensity or such journey. 

In other words Epaphroditus were true GIGACHAD. Deserving to be respected.

But because church sent him to serve Paul until Paul's situation get's resolved. Without some explanations it would feel like Epaphroditus failed his mission, returning home without Paul.

That's why Paul gives extensive explanation of why he sent Epaphroditus back.

    * Epaphroditus were very consered about situation at home.
    * Paul also was conserned. Because church was disstressed enough knowing that Paul might get executed. But on top of that now, they've got the new about Epaphroditus that he is also diying. And maybe dead already.

    By the way it described I think epaphraditus got very ill on the way to Rome. And all the curch knew it that he is in very bad condition. And remember it is at least 7 weeks before an update can get to them.

    * So, Paul sends the guy back home with the letter. So that church might get releived, at least partly. Get instructed on some important topics at the moment. Get notified of Timothy's arrival. etc.

Epaphroditus didn't fail him mission. He succeeded and such people deserves to be honored(verse 29). He committed to the job knowing he might never even make it. He considered need of others above himself. He was ready to lay his life. He proved his CHRIST LIKE character by actions. We very well adheres to many points we discussed over the last few week. And 

### Philippians 2:29,30
> ²⁹...hold people like him in high regard,³⁰because he came close to death for the work of Christ, risking his life to compensate for your absence in your service to me.

This is the kind of people we need to aim to be. Who show PROVEN CHARACTER by doing not by words.
